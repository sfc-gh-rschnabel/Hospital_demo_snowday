# Allied Health Services Guidelines

## Document Information
- **Policy Number**: AH-001
- **Effective Date**: January 1, 2024
- **Review Date**: January 1, 2025
- **Department**: Allied Health Services
- **Approved By**: Dr. Robert Kim, VP of Clinical Services

## Purpose
To establish guidelines for the delivery of allied health services including physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, respiratory therapy, and other rehabilitation services within the hospital setting.

## Allied Health Disciplines

### Physical Therapy (PT)
**Scope of Practice**:
- Mobility assessment and training
- Gait training and assistive device fitting
- Therapeutic exercise prescription
- Pain management through physical modalities
- Post-surgical rehabilitation
- Fall prevention programs

**Credentials**: Licensed Physical Therapist (PT), Physical Therapist Assistant (PTA)

### Occupational Therapy (OT)
**Scope of Practice**:
- Activities of daily living (ADL) training
- Upper extremity rehabilitation
- Cognitive rehabilitation
- Adaptive equipment assessment
- Home safety evaluation
- Return-to-work assessment

**Credentials**: Licensed Occupational Therapist (OT), Occupational Therapy Assistant (OTA)

### Speech-Language Pathology (SLP)
**Scope of Practice**:
- Swallowing assessment and dysphagia management
- Speech and language rehabilitation
- Cognitive-communication therapy
- Voice therapy
- Tracheostomy and ventilator communication
- Modified barium swallow studies

**Credentials**: Licensed Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP), Certificate of Clinical Competence (CCC-SLP)

### Respiratory Therapy (RT)
**Scope of Practice**:
- Oxygen therapy management
- Mechanical ventilation
- Airway management
- Pulmonary function testing
- Bronchial hygiene therapy
- Patient/family education

**Credentials**: Licensed Respiratory Therapist (RRT), Certified Respiratory Therapist (CRT)

### Registered Dietitian (RD)
**Scope of Practice**:
- Nutritional assessment
- Medical nutrition therapy
- Enteral/parenteral nutrition management
- Diabetes education
- Weight management counseling
- Food service coordination

**Credentials**: Registered Dietitian (RD), Certified Diabetes Educator (CDE)

## Referral Process

### Physician Orders Required
All allied health services require a physician order including:
- Patient name and identifiers
- Service type requested
- Diagnosis/indication
- Specific goals (if any)
- Frequency and duration
- Precautions or restrictions

### Referral Priority Levels
| Priority | Response Time | Indications |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Emergent | Within 1 hour | Airway issues, acute aspiration risk |
| Urgent | Within 4 hours | ICU patients, post-surgical day 0 |
| Routine | Within 24 hours | New admissions, scheduled evaluations |
| Follow-up | Within 48 hours | Reassessment, ongoing treatment |

## Assessment and Documentation

### Initial Evaluation
Completed within 24 hours of referral, including:
- Patient history and prior functional status
- Current functional assessment
- Standardized assessment tools (as applicable)
- Patient/family goals
- Treatment plan with measurable goals
- Anticipated duration of treatment
- Discharge recommendations

### Progress Notes
Documentation required:
- Daily notes for acute care
- Weekly summaries for rehabilitation
- Discharge summary upon service completion

### Outcome Measures
Standardized assessments used:
- **PT**: FIM scores, Timed Up and Go, Berg Balance Scale
- **OT**: FIM scores, Modified Barthel Index, AMPS
- **SLP**: FOIS (Functional Oral Intake Scale), MASA
- **RT**: Ventilator weaning protocols, bronchospasm scores

## Treatment Standards

### Treatment Frequency
| Setting | Typical Frequency |
|---------|-------------------|
| ICU | 1-2x daily as tolerated |
| Acute Care | 1x daily, 5-7 days/week |
| Rehabilitation | 3 hours/day minimum |
| Outpatient | 2-3x weekly |

### Treatment Duration Guidelines
| Service | Average Duration |
|---------|------------------|
| PT evaluation | 45-60 minutes |
| PT treatment | 30-45 minutes |
| OT evaluation | 45-60 minutes |
| OT treatment | 30-45 minutes |
| SLP evaluation | 30-45 minutes |
| SLP treatment | 30 minutes |
| RD assessment | 30-45 minutes |

## Patient and Family Education

### Education Components
- Disease/condition explanation
- Treatment rationale
- Home exercise programs (PT/OT)
- Dietary modifications (RD)
- Swallowing precautions (SLP)
- Equipment use training
- Safety precautions

### Education Documentation
- Topics covered
- Teaching methods used
- Patient/family understanding assessed
- Materials provided
- Follow-up needs identified

## Discharge Planning

### Discharge Criteria
- Goals achieved or plateau reached
- Safe for discharge destination
- Equipment/services arranged
- Patient/family educated
- Follow-up appointments scheduled

### Post-Discharge Recommendations
- Home health therapy if indicated
- Outpatient therapy referrals
- Equipment prescriptions
- Community resources
- Return precautions

## Quality and Performance Metrics

### Service Metrics
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Referral-to-evaluation time | < 24 hours |
| Treatment sessions per patient day | ≥ 1.0 |
| Patient satisfaction | ≥ 90% |
| Goals met at discharge | ≥ 80% |
| Documentation compliance | 100% |

### Patient Outcomes
| Outcome | Target |
|---------|--------|
| Functional improvement | Measurable gains |
| Discharge to home | ≥ 70% |
| Fall rate during therapy | 0 |
| Aspiration rate (SLP) | Baseline or improved |

## Billing and Coding

### Documentation Requirements for Billing
- Medical necessity established
- Treatment goals documented
- Skilled intervention required
- Progress toward goals documented
- Minutes of treatment recorded
