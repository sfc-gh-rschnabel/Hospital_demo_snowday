# Hospital Admission Policy and Procedures

- **Effective Date**: January 1, 2024
- **Review Date**: January 1, 2025
- **Department**: Patient Access Services
- **Approved By**: Dr. Sarah Chen, Chief Medical Officer

## Purpose
This policy establishes standardized procedures for patient admissions to ensure efficient, safe, and patient-centered care from the moment of arrival through bed assignment.

## Scope
This policy applies to all admission types including:
- Emergency admissions via the Emergency Department
- Elective/scheduled admissions
- Direct admissions from physician offices
- Transfers from other healthcare facilities
- Observation status admissions

## Admission Types

### Emergency Admissions
Emergency admissions are processed through the Emergency Department (ED) and have the highest priority for bed assignment. The target time from ED decision-to-admit to bed assignment is 4 hours (NEAT compliance target ≥90%).

**Process:**
1. ED physician determines need for admission
2. Admitting orders entered into the EHR system
3. Bed Management notified via electronic alert
4. Patient registered in ADT system
5. Insurance verification initiated
6. Bed assigned based on clinical needs and availability
7. Patient transported to assigned unit

### Elective Admissions
Elective admissions are pre-scheduled and require:
- Pre-admission testing completion
- Insurance pre-authorization (minimum 48 hours prior)
- Pre-operative clearance for surgical cases
- Assigned admission date and time

**Process:**
1. Surgeon/physician office schedules admission
2. Pre-admission services contacts patient
3. Insurance authorization obtained
4. Patient arrives at designated time
5. Registration completed
6. Pre-assigned bed confirmed available
7. Patient escorted to unit

## Bed Assignment Criteria

Bed assignments are made based on:
1. **Clinical Acuity**: ICU, Step-down, Medical/Surgical, Observation
2. **Specialty Requirements**: Cardiac monitoring, isolation, pediatric
3. **Infection Control Status**: Isolation requirements, immunocompromised
4. **Gender-Appropriate Placement**: Semi-private room considerations
5. **Special Equipment Needs**: Bariatric, ventilator, telemetry

## Required Documentation

The following documentation must be completed within timeframes:

| Document | Timeframe | Responsible Party |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Admission History & Physical | Within 24 hours | Admitting Physician |
| Nursing Assessment | Within 4 hours | Assigned RN |
| Medication Reconciliation | Within 4 hours | Pharmacist/RN |
| Fall Risk Assessment | Upon admission | Assigned RN |
| Skin Assessment | Upon admission | Assigned RN |
| Nutritional Screening | Within 24 hours | Dietary Services |

## Insurance and Financial

### Insurance Verification
- Verify insurance eligibility within 2 hours of admission
- Obtain authorization for admission within 24 hours
- Document authorization number in patient record
- Notify Case Management of high-cost admissions

### Self-Pay Patients
- Financial counseling offered within 24 hours
- Payment plan options discussed
- Charity care eligibility assessed
- Medicaid application assistance provided if applicable

## Patient Rights

Upon admission, patients receive:
- Written notice of Patient Rights and Responsibilities
- HIPAA Privacy Notice
- Advance Directive information
- Language interpreter services (if needed)
- Accommodation of cultural/religious preferences

## Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Time to Bed Assignment (ED) | < 4 hours | NEAT compliance |
| Admission Documentation Complete | 100% within 24 hours | Chart review |
| Insurance Verification | < 2 hours | Registration audit |
| Patient Satisfaction | ≥ 85% | Survey scores |
