# Patient Discharge Guidelines

## Document Information
- **Policy Number**: DIS-001
- **Effective Date**: January 1, 2024
- **Review Date**: January 1, 2025
- **Department**: Care Coordination
- **Approved By**: Dr. Patricia Davis, Chief Quality Officer

## Purpose
To ensure safe, efficient, and patient-centered discharge processes that minimize readmission risk and support successful transitions of care.

## Discharge Planning

### Early Discharge Planning
Discharge planning begins at admission:
- Expected length of stay documented within 24 hours
- Discharge needs assessment completed
- Case Management notified for complex patients
- Social Work referral if indicated

### Discharge Readiness Criteria
Patients are ready for discharge when:
1. Clinical stability achieved
2. Oral medications tolerated (if applicable)
3. Ambulating at baseline (or arrangements made)
4. Pain controlled with oral medications
5. Follow-up appointments scheduled
6. Post-discharge care arranged

## Discharge Process

### 24-48 Hours Before Discharge
1. Confirm discharge plan with patient/family
2. Schedule follow-up appointments
3. Arrange home health if needed
4. Complete medication reconciliation
5. Order discharge prescriptions
6. Arrange DME (durable medical equipment) if needed
7. Confirm transportation

### Day of Discharge
1. Physician writes discharge order
2. Nursing completes discharge teaching
3. Pharmacy reviews discharge medications
4. Patient demonstrates understanding of instructions
5. Prescriptions filled or provided
6. Wheelchair transport arranged
7. Patient physically leaves by target time

### Target Discharge Times
- **Morning Discharge Target**: 11:00 AM
- **Afternoon Discharge Target**: 2:00 PM
- **Goal**: 70% of discharges before 12:00 PM

## Discharge Documentation

### Required Elements
1. **Discharge Summary** (within 24 hours)
   - Admission diagnosis
   - Hospital course
   - Procedures performed
   - Discharge diagnosis
   - Condition at discharge
   - Discharge medications
   - Follow-up instructions

2. **Patient Instructions** (at discharge)
   - Diagnosis explanation in lay terms
   - Medication list with instructions
   - Activity restrictions
   - Diet recommendations
   - Warning signs to watch for
   - When to seek emergency care
   - Follow-up appointment details
   - Contact numbers for questions

3. **Medication Reconciliation**
   - Pre-admission medications
   - Medications started/changed during stay
   - Discharge medications
   - Medications discontinued
   - Patient/caregiver education completed

## High-Risk Patient Identification

### Readmission Risk Factors
Patients flagged for enhanced discharge planning:
- Prior 30-day readmission
- Heart failure diagnosis
- COPD exacerbation
- Diabetes with complications
- Multiple chronic conditions (≥3)
- Age ≥65 with complex needs
- Poor social support
- History of non-compliance
- Substance use disorder

### Enhanced Discharge Protocol
For high-risk patients:
1. Teach-back method for all instructions
2. Medication management support arranged
3. Follow-up call within 48-72 hours
4. PCP appointment within 7 days
5. Specialist follow-up within 14 days
6. Home health referral considered
7. Care transition coach assigned

## Specific Discharge Pathways

### Cardiac Surgery Discharge
- Incision care instructions
- Activity progression schedule
- Cardiac rehabilitation referral
- Anticoagulation management
- When to call surgeon
- Cardiologist follow-up in 2 weeks
- Surgeon follow-up in 4-6 weeks

### Joint Replacement Discharge
- Physical therapy home exercises
- DVT prophylaxis education
- Wound care instructions
- Weight bearing restrictions
- Outpatient PT scheduled
- Surgeon follow-up in 2 weeks

### Stroke Discharge
- Medication adherence education
- Stroke warning signs (FAST)
- Rehabilitation arrangements
- Modified Rankin Scale documented
- Neurology follow-up in 2-4 weeks
- Speech/OT/PT as indicated

### Heart Failure Discharge
- Daily weight monitoring education
- Fluid restriction review
- Low-sodium diet teaching
- Medication optimization confirmed
- Cardiology follow-up in 7 days
- Heart failure clinic referral

## Post-Discharge Follow-up

### Telephone Follow-up
- All inpatients: Call within 72 hours
- High-risk patients: Call within 24-48 hours
- Assess: Medication adherence, warning signs, questions
- Document in patient chart

### 30-Day Readmission Prevention
- Care Transitions Program enrollment
- Community health worker visits if indicated
- Medication therapy management referral
- Connection to community resources

## Discharge Against Medical Advice (AMA)

### AMA Process
1. Physician discussion with patient documented
2. Risks of leaving explained
3. AMA form signed by patient
4. Prescriptions offered for immediate safety
5. Follow-up information provided
6. No delay if patient insists on leaving
7. Attending physician notified

## Special Populations

### Pediatric Discharge
- Parent/guardian education required
- Car seat safety if under 2 years
- Pediatrician appointment scheduled
- School/daycare instructions provided

### Geriatric Discharge
- Cognitive assessment documented
- Fall risk mitigation addressed
- Caregiver identified and educated
- Elder services referral if indicated
- Medication simplification when possible

### Psychiatric Discharge
- Safety assessment completed
- Outpatient appointment within 7 days
- Crisis hotline number provided
- Medication adherence plan
- Family/support system notified with consent
