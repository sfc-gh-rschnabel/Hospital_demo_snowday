## Purpose
To ensure safe, accurate, and timely medication administration to all patients through standardized processes, verification procedures, and documentation requirements.

## The Five Rights of Medication Administration

Before administering any medication, verify:
1. **Right Patient**: Two patient identifiers (name and DOB/MRN)
2. **Right Drug**: Verify medication name matches order
3. **Right Dose**: Confirm dose calculation is correct
4. **Right Route**: Oral, IV, IM, subcutaneous, topical, etc.
5. **Right Time**: Within acceptable administration window

## Medication Order Requirements

### Valid Orders Must Include
- Patient name and identifiers
- Date and time of order
- Medication name (generic preferred)
- Dose with units of measurement
- Route of administration
- Frequency/schedule
- Duration (if applicable)
- Prescriber signature/authentication
- Indication (for PRN and high-alert medications)

### Unacceptable Order Practices
- Verbal orders (except emergencies)
- "Resume home meds" without specifics
- Trailing zeros (10.0 mg)
- Missing leading zeros (.5 mg)
- Abbreviations on Do-Not-Use list
- Range orders without clear titration parameters

## High-Alert Medications

### Categories Requiring Extra Verification
1. **Anticoagulants**: Heparin, warfarin, enoxaparin, DOACs
2. **Insulin**: All formulations
3. **Opioids**: Morphine, fentanyl, hydromorphone, oxycodone
4. **Neuromuscular Blocking Agents**: Rocuronium, succinylcholine
5. **Chemotherapy**: All cytotoxic agents
6. **Concentrated Electrolytes**: Potassium chloride, hypertonic saline
7. **IV Sedatives**: Propofol, midazolam, ketamine

### High-Alert Medication Protocol
- Independent double-check by two licensed personnel
- Barcode verification at bedside
- Patient weight verified for weight-based dosing
- Calculator used for all dose calculations
- Pre-administration assessment documented

## Controlled Substance Management

### Schedule II-V Medications
- Stored in locked automated dispensing cabinet (ADC)
- Waste witnessed and documented
- Count verified at shift change
- Discrepancies reported immediately
- Random audits conducted monthly

### Documentation Requirements
| Action | Documentation | Who |
|--------|--------------|-----|
| Removal from ADC | Time, patient, dose, RN ID | Administering RN |
| Administration | Time, site, patient response | Administering RN |
| Waste | Amount, witness ID | Two licensed personnel |
| Count Discrepancy | Incident report, pharmacy notification | Charge RN |

## Medication Administration Timing

### Time-Critical Medications
Administer within 30 minutes of scheduled time:
- Antibiotics
- Anticoagulants
- Insulin
- Immunosuppressants
- Pain medications (scheduled)
- Anticonvulsants

### Non-Time-Critical Medications
Administer within 1-2 hours of scheduled time:
- Vitamins
- Stool softeners
- Non-urgent scheduled medications

## PRN Medication Administration

### Requirements Before Administration
1. Assessment of indication documented
2. Appropriate time since last dose verified
3. Contraindications reviewed
4. Patient educated on expected effects

### Follow-up Requirements
- Pain medications: Reassess within 30-60 minutes
- Anti-anxiety: Reassess within 1 hour
- Anti-nausea: Reassess within 30 minutes
- Bowel medications: Reassess within appropriate timeframe

## IV Medication Guidelines

### Smart Pump Requirements
- All IV medications infused via smart pump
- Drug library utilized for dose limits
- Hard stops cannot be overridden
- Soft stops require clinical justification

### IV Compatibility
- Check compatibility before Y-site administration
- Use separate lines for incompatible medications
- Flush between incompatible medications
- Document compatibility verification

## Medication Reconciliation

### Admission
- Obtain complete medication history
- Verify with pharmacy records, pill bottles, caregivers
- Document home medications in EHR
- Reconcile with admission orders
- Address discrepancies with provider

### Discharge
- Compare discharge medications to admission list
- Identify new medications, changed doses, discontinued medications
- Provide patient education on all changes
- Document patient understanding

## Allergies and Adverse Reactions

### Allergy Documentation
- Document in designated allergy section
- Include reaction type (true allergy vs. intolerance)
- Distinguish anaphylaxis from side effects
- Update across all hospital systems

### Adverse Drug Event Reporting
- Report immediately to physician
- Document in patient record
- Complete safety event report
- Notify pharmacy for investigation
- FDA MedWatch report if required

## Storage and Handling

### Temperature Requirements
- Room temperature: 20-25°C (68-77°F)
- Refrigerated: 2-8°C (36-46°F)
- Freezer: -25 to -10°C (-13 to 14°F)

### Light-Sensitive Medications
- Store in amber containers or light-protective covers
- Minimize light exposure during administration
- Examples: Nitroglycerin, nitroprusside, amphotericin B

### Beyond-Use Dating
- Single-dose vials: Use immediately or within 6 hours
- Multi-dose vials: Per manufacturer guidance (28 days typical)
- Compounded products: Per pharmacy dating
