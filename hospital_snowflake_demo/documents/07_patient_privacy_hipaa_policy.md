- **Approved By**: Chief Privacy Officer

## Purpose
To ensure compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and protect the privacy and security of all protected health information (PHI) created, received, maintained, or transmitted by the hospital.

## Protected Health Information (PHI)

### Definition
PHI includes any individually identifiable health information relating to:
- Past, present, or future physical or mental health condition
- Healthcare provided to an individual
- Past, present, or future payment for healthcare

### PHI Identifiers (18 HIPAA Identifiers)
1. Names
2. Geographic data (smaller than state)
3. Dates (except year) related to individual
4. Phone numbers
5. Fax numbers
6. Email addresses
7. Social Security numbers
8. Medical record numbers
9. Health plan beneficiary numbers
10. Account numbers
11. Certificate/license numbers
12. Vehicle identifiers
13. Device identifiers
14. Web URLs
15. IP addresses
16. Biometric identifiers
17. Full-face photographs
18. Any other unique identifier

## Minimum Necessary Standard

### Use and Disclosure
- Access only the minimum PHI necessary to perform job duties
- Role-based access controls enforced
- Need-to-know basis for all PHI access
- Document justification for expanded access

### Workforce Requirements
- Access limited to job-specific needs
- Regular access audits conducted
- Access terminated immediately upon role change
- Training on minimum necessary standard

## Patient Rights

### Right to Access
- Patients may request copies of their medical records
- Response within 30 days (one 30-day extension permitted)
- Reasonable cost-based fees may apply
- Electronic format available upon request

### Right to Amendment
- Patients may request amendment of their records
- Response within 60 days
- May deny if information is accurate and complete
- Written denial with appeal process provided

### Right to Accounting of Disclosures
- Patients may request list of disclosures
- Covers 6 years prior to request
- Excludes treatment, payment, operations disclosures
- Response within 60 days

### Right to Request Restrictions
- Patients may request restrictions on uses/disclosures
- Hospital not required to agree (except for self-pay)
- Self-pay patients can restrict disclosure to insurers
- Agreed restrictions must be honored

### Right to Confidential Communications
- Patients may request alternative communication methods
- Accommodate reasonable requests
- Examples: Different phone number, mail to alternate address

## Permitted Uses and Disclosures

### Without Patient Authorization
- Treatment: Healthcare providers involved in care
- Payment: Insurance claims, billing activities
- Healthcare Operations: Quality improvement, training
- Public Health: Reporting requirements, disease surveillance
- Law Enforcement: Court orders, subpoenas (with notice)
- Emergencies: Prevent serious harm to individual or public

### Requiring Patient Authorization
- Marketing communications
- Sale of PHI
- Psychotherapy notes (most disclosures)
- Research (unless waived by IRB)
- Uses not covered by Notice of Privacy Practices

## Security Safeguards

### Administrative Safeguards
- Designated Privacy Officer and Security Officer
- Workforce training and management
- Security policies and procedures
- Sanctions for violations
- Regular risk assessments

### Physical Safeguards
- Facility access controls
- Workstation security
- Device and media controls
- Secure disposal of PHI

### Technical Safeguards
- Unique user identification
- Automatic logoff
- Encryption of electronic PHI
- Audit controls and monitoring
- Integrity verification

## Workforce Training

### Initial Training
- All workforce members within 30 days of hire
- Role-specific training as applicable
- Documentation of training completion

### Ongoing Training
- Annual refresher training
- Policy change updates
- Incident-driven training as needed

### Training Topics
- PHI definition and handling
- Minimum necessary standard
- Patient rights
- Breach prevention
- Reporting violations
- Security practices

## Violations and Sanctions

### Categories of Violations
1. **Unknowing**: Not aware, reasonable diligence
2. **Reasonable Cause**: Not willful neglect
3. **Willful Neglect - Corrected**: Corrected within 30 days
4. **Willful Neglect - Not Corrected**: Not corrected

### Internal Sanctions
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Suspension
- Termination
- Retraining requirements

## Business Associates

### Requirements
- Business Associate Agreement (BAA) required
- Same PHI protections as hospital
- Breach reporting obligations
- Subcontractor management

### Monitoring
- Annual review of agreements
- Incident tracking and response
- Termination provisions for violations
